Bara Imambara

Bada Imambara, also known as the Asfi Imambara, is a historic monument located in Lucknow, India. It is a grand Shia Muslim congregation hall built in 1784 by Asaf-ud-Daula, the Nawab of Awadh. The complex is a marvel of Mughal architecture, featuring a massive hall with a high ceiling, intricate designs, and ornate decorations. The building is famous for its unique labyrinthine passages, known as the "Bhool Bhulaiya," which are a major tourist attraction. The Imambara complex also houses a mosque, a stepwell, and a beautiful garden, making it a popular cultural and historical destination. The complex is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of Lucknow and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, architecture, and culture. The intricate designs and ornate decorations of the complex are a reflection of the rich craftsmanship of the artisans of that era.

Hazratganj Market

Hazratganj is a downtown and main shopping hub of Lucknow, the capital and largest city of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. Founded in 1827 by Nawab Nasir-ud-Din Haidar Shah, it has a rich history and has evolved over time to become a major Victorian-style shopping area. Today, it houses showrooms, shopping complexes, restaurants, hotels, cafes, theatres, offices, and businesses, selling everything from the famous Lucknow Chikan material to goods from around the world. The market is also home to several cultural events and programmes, including a monthly carnival on the second Sunday of each month, which attracts large crowds and adds to the vibrant atmosphere of the area.

Lucknow zoo

Irrespective of the name, the Lucknow Zoo has been a favourite haunt over the years. Its sprawling gardens with the tall palm trees and the museum with it mummies has been an attraction for both the young and the old. Developed as a mango orchard in the 18th century by Nawab Nasiruddin Haider, the then Nawab of Awadh the place was named Banarasi Bagh during those times, and is still referred to by the same even today.

Ambedkar park

Ambedkar Memorial Park is a public park and memorial located in Gomti Nagar, Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India. The park is dedicated to Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar, the chief architect of the Indian Constitution. The park was constructed by the Mayawati government and was inaugurated in 2009. It is a 107-acre park that features a stunning memorial, a museum, and beautifully landscaped gardens. The park is designed to promote social harmony and equality, reflecting the values of Dr. Ambedkar. The memorial features a 24-meter-tall bronze statue of Dr. Ambedkar, as well as statues of other prominent Dalit leaders. The park also hosts various cultural events and programmes throughout the year, making it a popular tourist destination in Lucknow.

Lulu Mall

Lulu Mall Lucknow is a massive shopping mall located in Lucknow, India. Spanning 45.9 acres, it is one of the largest malls in India with a total built-up area of 19 lakh square feet. The mall features over 200 national and international brands, offering a wide range of products and services. The mall has a large food court with multiple dining options, a cinema complex with 11 screens, and an entertainment zone with a variety of games and activities. Additionally, the mall has a hypermarket, a department store, and a separate section for electronics and furniture. The mall also provides ample parking space for over 7,000 vehicles and is equipped with state-of-the-art security and surveillance systems.
